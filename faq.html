FAQ's

What is polymer clay?

Polymer clay is a water-resistant, ultra light and soft material. It’s a manmade clay and is widely used in the jewellery making industry. When cured properly, the clay will bend rather than snap, and won’t weigh your ears down when you go for beautifully large and quirky pair on your evening out!

How should I look after my earrings?

You can easily remove dirt and makeup from clay components by GENTLY going over dirty spots with a q-tip dipped in rubbing alcohol or acetone. Metal components may tarnish and darken over time if exposed to water and oils from the skin, but can be easily cleaned with a soft cloth and/or tarnish remover.

Although these polymer earrings are flexible please avoid bending the clay, scratching the surface, or pulling roughly on the components as some components can be delicate.

Try to store your earrings separately to avoid deterioration over time, rubbing next to other earrings can discolour or transfer oils onto your new pair!

What materials do you use?

All earrings contain polymer clay, and are sometimes accented with gold-plated or brass charms to add a little sass to your elegant ears.

Why doesn’t my pair look exactly like the photograph?

All items are handmade, and therefore some small imperfections/inconsistencies may occur. Examples of these may include very small bubbles, slight variances of colour from what appears on screen, or other small marks that in no way affect the functionality or overall aesthetic appeal of an item. I will ask you to provide a photograph of the earrings if you would like to process a return.
